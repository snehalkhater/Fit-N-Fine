Renegade Row

Renegade Rows are a compound, full-body exercise that builds back and core strength, improves stability and balance, and enhances posture by engaging muscles like the lats, rhomboids, and obliques while holding a stable plank position. This challenging move requires tight core control to prevent rotation and improve overall functional fitness, making it effective for strengthening the upper body and core simultaneously.

How to Choose Renegade Row Workouts

Full-Body Strength: The exercise combines a pulling motion with a plank, working multiple muscle groups, including the back (lats, rhomboids), arms (biceps, triceps), and core.
Enhanced Core Stability: By demanding anti-rotational strength to keep the hips and back stable, renegade rows significantly strengthen the core muscles (abs, obliques).
Improved Balance and Coordination: The need to stabilize the body on an unstable surface while performing a single-arm row helps develop superior balance and coordination.
Better Posture: Strengthening the upper back and core muscles supports better posture and can counteract the effects of prolonged sitting or hunching.
